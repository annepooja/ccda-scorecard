C-CDA R2.1 Scorecard

(Alpha Release)

Introduction to the Scorecard

The Scorecard tool has been developed to help improve interoperability of Consolidated Clinical Document Architecture (C-CDA) documents by providing a means to test the C-CDA documents more rigorously, beyond the requirements of 2015 Edition Health IT Certification Regulation. This is an enhancement of the tool initially developed by the SMART team from Harvard. Health Level 7 (HL7) Standards Development Organization, which maintains the C-CDA Standard, has developed the rules and scoring criteria used in the tool. Please click here to view the scoring criteria. We hope that providers and health IT developers will use the tool to identify and resolve issues of C-CDA document interoperability among disparate systems.

Interpreting Scorecard Results

The scorecard tool provides two scores:

  1. Certification Score
  2. Grade

The "Certification Score" indicates whether the C-CDA document meets the requirements of the 2015 Edition Health IT Certification and the result is indicated simply as "Pass" or "Fail".

Additionally, each C-CDA document is scored and graded for set of enhanced interoperability rules developed by HL7. A higher score (grade) means that information is coded with appropriate structure and semantics and hence has a better chance of interoperating with disparate system. The maximum score is 100 points and the grades are:

  • A+ (more than 94 points)
  • A- (90 to 94 points)
  • B+ (85 to 89 points)
  • B- (80 to 84 points)
  • C (70 to 79 points)
  • D (less than 70 points)
